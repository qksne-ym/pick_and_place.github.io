Introduction

Pick-and-place tasks are critically important in both industrial production and everyday life. Their applications span virtually every scenario requiring the movement of objects.

In these scenarios, robotic grasping and placement often cannot be achieved through simple linear back-and-forth motions, particularly in complex, unstructured environments like agricultural fields, laboratories, and homes. These settings demand robots to perceive and understand their surroundings, avoiding obstacles to complete grasp-and-place tasks. Therefore, given the importance and widespread application of grasp-and-place tasks, research into autonomous execution of such tasks holds significant value.
